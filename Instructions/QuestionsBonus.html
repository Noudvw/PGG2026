In this experiment, we will ask you to make some estimates. In some cases, we ask you to report the option that is most likely to you (e.g. how many tokens does player X contribute to the project?). In other cases, we ask you to report the probability that something will happen (e.g. what is the probability that player X contributes Y tokens to the project?).

Please report truthfully when asked to make an estimate. You can earn a bonus of 4 points for one randomly-selected estimate (out of a total of 9 estimates). Reporting honestly maximizes the chance of earning the bonus, for more information you can click the button below. If you don't need more information, please answer the questions at the bottom of the page.

We ask estimates in two stages. In stage 1, you are asked to report the most likely option. As an example: let's say you are asked to estimate how much player X will contribute to the group project. Let's say you pick the number Y. This is your first answer: player X will contribute Y tokens.
In the second stage, we ask you for a percent chance that your prediction is correct (and player X will actually contribute Y tokens). We will ask you for a percentage (P). At the same time, a computer randomly generates a number N from 1 to 100.
If N is smaller than your reported chance P, you will earn points if your prediction is true. (In other words, if player X indeed contributed Y tokens). If N is greater than or equal to your reported chance P, you will earn the bonus with N percent chance.
This means that, to increase your chance of winning the 4 bonus Points, you should (1) predict the Y that is most likely to occur, and (2) report the percent chance P that you believe is accurate
