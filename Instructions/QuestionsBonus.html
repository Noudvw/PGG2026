In this experiment, we will ask you to make some estimates. In some cases, we ask you to report the option that is most likely to you (e.g. "how many tokens does player X contribute to the project?"). In other cases, we ask you to report the probability that something will happen (e.g. "what is the probability that player X contributes Y tokens to the project?").

Please report truthfully when asked to make an estimate. You can earn a bonus of 4 Points for one randomly-selected estimate (out of a total of 9 estimates). Reporting honestly maximizes the chance of earning the bonus. For detailed information on how honest reporting increases your odds of earning the 4 Points bonus, click the button below. If you don't need more information, please answer the questions at the bottom of the page.

You are asked to report estimates in two stages. In stage 1, you are asked to make an estimate about choices that others make during the game. In the stage 2, you are asked for a percent chance (P) that your prediction from stage 1 is correct. At the same time, a computer randomly generates a number (N) from 1 to 100. Your chances of winning the bonus depend on your reported P and the randomly-generated N.

If N is smaller or equal to your reported chance P, you will earn points if your prediction (about the other player's behaviour) is true.
If N is greater than your reported chance P, you will earn the bonus with N percent chance.

This means that, to increase your chance of winning the 4 bonus Points, you should
(1) predict the choices that other players are most likely to make, and
(2) report the percent chance P that they will do the action stated at (1)


Bonus Points - Example

- You make multiple estimates throughout the experiment.
- The estimate about player B's contribution is randomly selected for payment.

- You report that player B contributes 7 tokens.
- You report to be 37% certain about your estimate (about player B contributing 7 tokens).
- A random number (N) is drawn

Two Options
- N exceeds or equals 37%. This means that you have a N% chance of winning the bonus Points for the estimate.
- N is lower than 37%. This means that you get the bonus Points if your estimate is correct (and player B contributed 7 Points).
